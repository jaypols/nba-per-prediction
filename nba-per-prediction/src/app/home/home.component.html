Predicting NBA Players PER

PER (Player Efficiency Rating) represents a player's per-minute productivity.

Machine Learning

Utilizes a Random Forest algorithm consisting of many decision trees.

Similarity Scores

Current Players and Similar Players PER is displayed.

John Hollinger developed a forumala to find an all-in-one stat for a player, PER.
PER is important in determining how well a player performs by weighing:
  • positive stats
  • negative stats
  • pace of game
It is not a once and for all evaluation, but it is the best way to summarize a player's accomplishments.
The Random Forest produced an accuracy of 84.5%.
786 players with 3584 data rows were included in the training set.

Similarity Scores are a percentage based on similar quality, shape, position, and win shares in comparison to the current player.
This provides a visual representation of the predicted PER and the similar player's PER.
All stats used from basketball-reference.com
